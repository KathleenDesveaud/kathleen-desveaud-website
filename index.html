Research & Publications

My research explores artificial intelligence from two complementary perspectives, examining both individual user experiences and organizational implementation strategies. This dual approach provides comprehensive insights into how AI technologies are reshaping marketing practices and consumer behavior.

Consumer Perspective

I study how individuals perceive, adopt, or reject AI technologies. This includes issues related to trust, appropriation, and representations of automation in everyday life, focusing on the psychological and behavioral aspects of human-AI interaction.

Business Perspective

I examine how organizations incorporate data and AI into their marketing practices, including process transformation, ethical challenges, and customer relationship dynamics within the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence.

Research Focus Areas

Consumer Trust & Algorithmic Decisions

Understanding how users build trust in AI-driven systems and automated decision-making processes.

Voice Assistants & Human-Machine Interaction

Exploring the dynamics of conversational AI and its impact on user behavior and engagement.

Brand Loyalty Modeling & Consumer Psychology

Analyzing psychological factors that drive brand attachment and loyalty in digital environments.

Gender, Identity & AI Embodiment

Investigating how gender representation in AI affects user perceptions and interaction patterns.

Data Ethics & Algorithmic Bias

Examining ethical implications of AI systems and addressing bias in algorithmic decision-making.

Vision & Approach

My research philosophy centers on the conviction that artificial intelligence must be understood, mastered, and deployed ethically and responsibly. I firmly believe that technology should serve humanity, not the other way around.

In my pedagogical approach, I emphasize hands-on learning and critical thinking. My students learn not only Big Data and Machine Learning techniques but also to question the ethical and societal implications of these technologies.
